How would you describe who you are as a person? By the work you do? By the hobbies you take part in? By your personality? 
Or more realistically by some combination of the above.  

## What makes you, you

Everyone sees you differently. To some people you are kind and to others selfish. To some people you are interesting and to others boring. 
Maybe the best way you can describe youself is how you perceive youself but that doesn't do the question justice.

Maybe you think the very question itself is bad because it's vague and not properly defined. But I think that vagueness is what
makes the question interesting. I think the question teaches us that there is no clear ratio of career, hobbies, and
personality that makes you, you. 

## Nuanced answer

There are so many sides to a person that it's impossible to capture them all in a single answer. You are the person you are at work, 
the person you are with your friends, the person you are with your family, and the person you are when you're alone. 
You are the person you were in the past, the person you are in the present, and the person you will be in the future.

I think as a result the question is best answered not by words but through experience. Only by spending time with the person can you understand who they
really are. Only by creating memories with them can you really find an answer to the question - which I think is beautiful. The next time someone
asks you what kind of person you are, tell them to spend time with you and find out for themselves.
